# Win Report Output Format — Annotated Guide

## The Canonical Example (from real output)

Study this line by line. This IS the target output format.

```
We have a new WGMS sale - Large $499
Ticket URL: https://duplicator.wpsiteassist.com/conversation/27453?folder_id=51
```

**Line 1:** Always starts with "We have a new WGMS sale -" then the tier label and price.
Tier labels: Small $149 | Large $299 | High-Volume (Complex) $499

**Line 2:** Ticket URL on its own line. If user didn't provide it, omit this line.

---

```
Let me share my experiments and how I win this ticket from a presale to pro license + wgms large
package conversion. Thanks to @Nik he shared this ticket to this channel and shared to be
transparent with live woocommerce migration data integrity. I followed that instruction to craft
the reply.
```

**Opening paragraph:** 1-3 sentences.
- "Let me share my experiments and how I win this ticket" — always include this phrase or close variant
- State the conversion arc: "from [X] to [Y]"
- If another team member was involved (shared the ticket, gave advice, set context), credit them with @mention
- If no collaboration, skip the thanks line

---

```
1st email, Reply Pattern:
* Ensuring our experience in this kind of complex migration task
* Data integrity and maintenance mode approach
* How do we audit and cost range for our service price
* License requirements - Duplicator pro plan
* How to proceed next
```

**Email section format:**
- Label: `[Nth] email, Reply Pattern:` — ordinals: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, Last attempt
- 3-6 bullet points, each a SHORT phrase (5-10 words max)
- Bullets describe the STRATEGIC INTENT of the reply, not the literal content
- Written as noun phrases or gerunds, not full sentences
- Think: "what was this reply trying to accomplish?"

**Good bullet examples:**
* Establishing credibility in complex migrations ✅
* Addressing data integrity concern proactively ✅
* Presenting cost range before quote ✅
* Outlining exactly what customer needs to do next ✅

**Bad bullet examples:**
* I told the customer that we have experience ❌ (too wordy, wrong voice)
* The customer had a WooCommerce site ❌ (description, not strategy)

---

```
2nd email, Reply pattern: Customer
* Presale converted here with Duplicator Pro - Pro license
* Came up with more questions regarding the migration
* Then he performed the migration on his own and fall into issues
```

**Customer reply section:**
When the email being described is a CUSTOMER message (not agent), add "Customer" after the label.
Use this to note what the customer did, decided, or experienced.
Still use bullets — brief, factual, story-advancing.

---

```
Last attempt email: As he already performed the migration by his own and falled into an issue.
The issue was with admin login not working. Here I think it differently and pitched him with
first help and offer migration approach:
* I observe each thing he replied with and craft with all acknowledgements
* Offered him support and ask for server access too
* What I did different here:
   * I checked the backup size from his FTP file shared and share the quote price directly in
     this email along with the solution mindset
   * Also, told him if you want it from scratch and no data loss then we can use our service.
   * Finally, the last sentence - Standard support can take back and forth communication and
     live site may be affect with this.
```

**"Last attempt" / decisive email section:**
This is the most important section. Format it differently:
- Start with a 1-2 sentence setup BEFORE the bullets: "As he already... Here I think it differently..."
- Use "Last attempt email:" as the label (no ordinal number)
- Standard bullets first (what was done)
- Then a nested "What I did different here:" sub-section with indented bullets
- The nested bullets are the GOLD — the transferable insights
- Keep the agent's authentic voice: "I checked", "I observed", "I think it differently"

---

```
These are all my approaches and thought process with AI and past experience. Finally converted
to the large wgms sale. Sharing with you as a case study, I hope you will get benefited from
it in other cases too.
```

**Closing paragraph:** Always end with these two sentences (adapt lightly):
- "[Summary of approach source]. Finally converted to the [size] wgms sale."
- "Sharing with you as a case study, I hope you will get benefited from it in other cases too."

Keep the slightly imperfect English — it's the authentic team voice.

## Full Template (Copy This)

```
We have a new WGMS sale - [SIZE] $[PRICE]
[Ticket URL: URL]

Let me share my experiments and how I win this ticket from [CONVERSION ARC]. [OPTIONAL: Thanks to @[Name] [reason].]

1st email, Reply Pattern:
* [Strategic intent]
* [Strategic intent]
* [Strategic intent]
* [Strategic intent]
* [Strategic intent]

[Nth] email, Reply pattern: [Customer if customer email]
* [What happened / strategic intent]
* [What happened / strategic intent]
* [What happened / strategic intent]

Last attempt email: [1-2 sentence context about the situation and mindset shift]. [Optional: "Here I think it differently and..."]
* [What was done]
* [What was done]
* What I did different here:
   * [The decisive tactic]
   * [The decisive tactic]
   * [The decisive tactic — often the final sentence or closing line used]

These are all my approaches and thought process with AI and past experience. Finally converted to the [size] wgms sale. Sharing with you as a case study, I hope you will get benefited from it in other cases too.
```

---

## What Makes a Great Win Report

1. **The arc is clear** — reader immediately understands the journey
2. **The bullets are strategic** — "why" not "what happened"
3. **The decisive move is visible** — "What I did different here" is explicit
4. **It's teachable** — a teammate reading it knows what to do next time
5. **It sounds human** — not corporate, not over-polished
6. **It's short** — can be read in 60 seconds
